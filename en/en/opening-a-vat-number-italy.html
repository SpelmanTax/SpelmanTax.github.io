How to Open a VAT Number in Italy (Partita IVA) for Freelancers

Starting a freelance activity in Italy often requires opening a VAT number (Partita IVA). This allows you to legally provide services, issue invoices, and pay taxes. This guide explains the basic steps for freelancers who want to work in Italy.

1. What is a VAT Number (Partita IVA)?

A Partita IVA is the tax identification number used by freelancers, professionals and businesses in Italy. It allows you to issue invoices, pay income tax and social security contributions, and operate legally as a self‑employed professional. Freelancers in Italy often choose the flat‑rate regime (Regime Forfettario) if they meet certain conditions.

2. Who needs a Partita IVA?

You usually need to open a VAT number if you work as a freelancer or consultant, provide services regularly, invoice clients independently, or work with international clients. Typical freelancers include developers, designers, consultants, marketers, translators and online professionals.

3. Choosing the tax regime

Many freelancers choose the Regime Forfettario, a simplified tax system for small businesses. It offers simplified accounting, reduced tax rates (5% for new activities, then 15%), and simplified VAT obligations. This regime is available if your annual revenue stays within specific limits.

4. Steps to open a VAT number in Italy

  • Choose the ATECO code (activity classification).
  • Register the activity with the Italian Tax Agency (Agenzia delle Entrate).
  • Choose the tax regime (e.g., Regime Forfettario).
  • Register with the social security system (INPS) if required.

A tax advisor can handle these steps for you.

5. Social security contributions

Freelancers must usually pay contributions to INPS. The amount depends on the type of activity and the chosen regime. These contributions are necessary to build pension rights in Italy.

6. Accounting and tax obligations

Freelancers with a Partita IVA must issue invoices, keep basic accounting records, submit an annual tax return, and pay income tax and social security contributions. A commercialista can help manage these obligations.
